## Academic Exposition & Historical Context

### 1. Linguistic Spelling Variations
The name of the Babylonian monarch is represented in ancient texts with spelling variations:
- **Hebrew/Aramaic**: נְבוּכַדְנֶאצַּר (*Nəḇūḵaḏneṣṣar*) is the dominant spelling in Daniel and Kings.
- **Alternative Hebrew Spelling**: נְבוּכַדְרֶאצַּר (*Nəḇūḵaḏreṣṣar*), featuring a 'r' instead of 'n' (Nebuchadrezzar), occurs primarily in the Book of Jeremiah (e.g. Jer 21:2) and is closer to the original Akkadian.
- **Akkadian**: *Nabû-kudurri-uṣur*, meaning "O Nabû, protect my firstborn son" or "O Nabû, protect my boundary stone."

### 2. The Campaign of 605 BCE
The first siege of Jerusalem in **605 BCE** occurred in the wake of the pivotal Battle of Carchemish, where crown prince Nebuchadnezzar defeated Pharaoh Necho II of Egypt, establishing Babylonian hegemony over the Levant. 

According to Daniel 1:1, this took place during the "third year" of Jehoiakim. While some commentators saw an apparent contradiction with Jeremiah 46:2 (which dates the Carchemish battle to Jehoiakim's fourth year), the chronological consensus (Edwin Thiele et al.) resolves this through the Babylonian accession-year system. Under this system, Jehoiakim's first official year began in the civil year *after* his appointment, making his "third year" of reign coincide with the fourth year of his actual tenure.

### 3. Chronological Succession of Sieges
The database details three distinct sieges:
1. **605 BCE (First Deportation)**: Jehoiakim submits, and hostages (including Daniel and his companions) are taken to Babylon.
2. **597 BCE (Second Deportation)**: Jehoiachin is deposed after a brief reign, and 10,000 captives (including Ezekiel and the elite of Judah) are deported.
3. **589-587 BCE (Destruction)**: Zedekiah's rebellion leads to the final, catastrophic siege, the destruction of Jerusalem and Solomon's Temple, and the third major deportation.
