# Small Group Discussion Questions: The New Perspective on Paul and Justification

**Persona**: Billy Graham / Compassionate Pastor  
**Date**: 2026-06-20  

These questions are designed to guide a small group or personal Bible study through the theological and practical realities of justification by faith in Romans 3-4 and Galatians 2-3, reflecting both traditional and New Perspective themes.

---

## Tier 1: Observation Questions (What does the text say?)
1.  Read **Galatians 2:15-16**. How many times does Paul repeat the word "justified" or "justify"? What is contrasted with "faith in Jesus Christ" in these verses?
2.  According to **Romans 3:21-22**, how has the righteousness of God been manifested? What role do the "Law and the Prophets" play in relation to this manifestation?
3.  Look at **Romans 3:27-28**. What does Paul say is "excluded"? By what kind of law or principle is it excluded?
4.  In **Romans 4:9-12**, when was faith credited to Abraham as righteousness? Was it before or after his circumcision? Why is this timing structurally important to Paul’s argument?

---

## Tier 2: Interpretation Questions (What does the text mean?)
1.  The traditional Protestant view translates *pistis Christou* as "faith in Christ" (our belief in Him), while many New Perspective scholars translate it as "the faithfulness of Christ" (His obedience on the cross). Read **Galatians 2:16** with both translations. How does each option shift the theological emphasis of the verse?
2.  How does the archaeological discovery of the phrase "works of the law" (*ma'aseh ha-torah*) in the Dead Sea Scrolls (4QMMT) help us understand what the Jews of Paul's day meant by this term? How does this inform the NPP argument that Paul is correcting ethnic exclusion rather than legalistic self-effort?
3.  Read **Romans 3:25-26**. Explain the meaning of the word "propitiation" (*hilastērion*) in light of the Old Testament mercy seat. How does Christ's death vindicate the justice and character of God?
4.  In **Galatians 2:20**, Paul speaks of being "crucified with Christ." What does it mean for a believer's daily life to have Christ living in them, rather than living under the legal demands of the Law?

---

## Tier 3: Application Questions (How does this apply to us?)
1.  Paul stood up to Peter in Antioch (Gal 2:11-14) because Peter withdrew from fellowship with Gentile believers. Are there people or groups in your community or church that you consciously or unconsciously distance yourself from? How does the doctrine of justification challenge these divisions?
2.  If justification is a free, forensic gift (Rom 3:24) that is completely independent of our performance, how does this truth change how you deal with failure, guilt, and professional anxiety in your daily life?
3.  We may not struggle with the Jewish law of circumcision, but what are some modern "boundary markers" or "works of the law" that Christians use today to prove they are "good Christians" or to judge others?
4.  How can our small group or church better reflect the multi-ethnic, inclusive covenant family of Abraham that God promised and Christ established? What concrete action steps can we take this week?
