## 2. Key Findings Summary

### Historical Development & Contemporary Context
*   **The Paradigm Shift**: E.P. Sanders ([037-topic.md](037-topic.md), [039-online.md](039-online.md)) challenged the traditional view of Second Temple Judaism as legalistic works-righteousness, proposing "Covenantal Nomism" where grace precedes law-keeping. Dunn ([028-keywords.md](028-keywords.md)) focused on "boundary markers" separating Jews from Gentiles, and Wright ([033-themes.md](033-themes.md)) framed justification as an ecclesiological announcement of covenant membership.
*   **Scholarly Backlash**: Reformed scholars like Carson, Moo, and Schreiner ([039-online.md](039-online.md)) defended individual, forensic justification and the imputation of Christ's righteousness, warning against a purely social or horizontal reading.

### Key Passages and Exegesis
*   **Galatians 2:15-21**: Grounded in the Antioch Incident ([029-nt-context.md](029-nt-context.md)). Paul argues that Jewish and Gentile believers must sit at the same table because both are justified solely by faith in Christ, not by *erga nomou* ([031-flow.md](031-flow.md)).
*   **Romans 3:20-28**: God's righteousness is manifested *apart from the law* through the faithfulness/faith in Christ, providing redemption and propitiation (*hilastērion*) for all who believe, eliminating boasting ([035-nt-meaning.md](035-nt-meaning.md)).
*   **Romans 4 & Galatians 3**: Abraham was justified by faith prior to circumcision, establishing him as the father of a single, multi-ethnic family ([032-nt-highlights.md](032-nt-highlights.md)).

### Contested Terminology
*   **ἔργα νόμου (*erga nomou*)**: Under the NPP, these are identity boundary markers (circumcision, diet, sabbath); under the traditional view, they refer to all attempts to keep the moral law to earn salvation ([028-keywords.md](028-keywords.md)). The Dead Sea Scroll parallel 4QMMT supports the identity/exclusion connotation ([036-insights.md](036-insights.md)).
*   **δικαιοσύνη θεοῦ (*dikaiosynē theou*)**: Under the NPP, it is God's covenant faithfulness; under the traditional view, it is the forensic righteousness imputed to the believer ([028-keywords.md](028-keywords.md)).
*   **πίστις Χριστοῦ (*pistis Christou*)**: Subjective genitive ("faithfulness of Christ") vs. objective genitive ("faith in Christ") ([028-keywords.md](028-keywords.md)).
