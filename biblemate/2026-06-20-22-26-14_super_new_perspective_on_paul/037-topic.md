# Topical Study: The New Perspective on Paul

**Persona**: Cambridge Theologian  
**Date**: 2026-06-20  

This study examines the New Perspective on Paul (NPP) as a major paradigm shift in Pauline studies, tracing the themes of covenant, law, and justification from the Old Testament through the New Testament.

---

## 1. Biblical-Theological Scope: Covenant and Law from Genesis to Revelation

### The Seed-Covenant in the Old Testament
*   **Genesis**: The covenant begins with the promise to Abraham (Gen 12:1-3; 15:1-6) that he would inherit the world and be the father of a multitude of nations. Circumcision is given as the covenant sign (Gen 17:11).
*   **Exodus to Deuteronomy**: The Mosaic Covenant (Sinai) is established. The Torah is given as the covenant treaty. It outlines a way of life for Israel to maintain its identity as a holy nation, separate from the surrounding Gentiles (Lev 11:44-45; Deut 7:6).
*   **The Prophets**: The prophets highlight Israel's failure to keep the covenant, leading to exile. They look forward to a **New Covenant** (Jer 31:31-34; Ezek 36:26-27) where the law will be written on the heart, the Spirit will be given, and sins will be forgiven.

### Second Temple Judaism and "Covenantal Nomism"
*   **E.P. Sanders' Breakthrough**: In *Paul and Palestinian Judaism* (1977), Sanders argued that Second Temple Judaism was not a system of self-righteous legalism (as the Reformers assumed). Instead, it was based on **Covenantal Nomism**: one gets "in" the covenant by God's grace and election, and one stays "in" the covenant by keeping the law (nomism). The law was kept out of gratitude, not to earn salvation.
*   **The Problem of Boasting**: If Judaism was a religion of grace, why did Paul attack it? The NPP answers: because the law had become an instrument of national privilege and ethnic exclusion. The "boasting" Paul attacks in Romans 3:27 is not the boasting of individual achievement, but the boasting of Jewish national privilege.

### The New Testament Fulfillment
*   **The Gospels**: Jesus confronts Jewish nationalism and the fence-building of the Pharisees, announcing that the Kingdom of God is open to all who repent and believe, regardless of ethnic heritage (Matt 8:11-12).
*   **Acts**: The Jerusalem Council (Acts 15) represents the historical transition where the church formally decides that Gentile believers do not need to be circumcised to be saved.
*   **The Pauline Epistles**: Romans and Galatians provide the theological rationale for this decision. Justification by faith alone is the mechanism by which the Abrahamic covenant is opened to the nations.
*   **Revelation**: The covenantal trajectory culminates in the new creation, where a multi-ethnic family of God from "every tribe, language, people, and nation" worships the Lamb (Rev 5:9; 7:9).

## 2. Linguistic Background

The theological debate rests on specific Greek translations of Hebrew concepts:

### Hebrew *Tsedaqah* to Greek *Dikaiosynē*
*   **Hebrew (*Tsedaqah*)**: In the Hebrew Bible, *tsedaqah* is a relational, covenantal term referring to God's faithfulness to His covenant promises (e.g., Isa 51:5).
*   **Greek (*Dikaiosynē*)**: In Classical Greek, it refers to the abstract virtue of justice. In the LXX and the NT, however, it takes on the covenantal coloring of the Hebrew *tsedaqah*. The NPP argues that *dikaiosynē theou* in Paul must be interpreted through this Hebrew lens as "God's covenant faithfulness."

### Hebrew *Ma'aseh* to Greek *Ergon*
*   **Hebrew (*Ma'aseh*)**: Actions or deeds.
*   **Greek (*Ergon*)**: In the NT phrase *erga nomou*, it translates the Hebrew *ma'aseh ha-torah* (as found in 4QMMT). The phrase designates specific actions prescribed by the Law of Moses, particularly those that functioned as social boundary markers.

## 3. Systematic Formulation and Theological Assessment

### Core Contributions of the NPP
1.  **Restoring Second Temple Context**: The NPP corrected the anachronism of reading the 16th-century debate between Protestants and Catholics (Luther vs. the Pope) directly back into the 1st-century conflict between Paul and the Judaizers.
2.  **Highlighting the Social Dimension**: The NPP restored the horizontal/ecclesiological dimension of justification, showing that it has immediate implications for the unity and inclusivity of the church.
3.  **Redemptive-Historical Depth**: The NPP connected Paul's soteriology directly to the covenantal storyline of the Old Testament.

### Limitations and Critical Concerns
1.  **The Individual and Vertical Dimension**: By emphasizing the horizontal boundary markers, the NPP sometimes downplays the vertical problem of the sinner's guilt and condemnation before a holy Judge (Romans 1:18-3:20). Justification is not just about "who is in the family," but "how a guilty sinner can stand before a holy God."
2.  **The Imputation of Righteousness**: The rejection of imputation in some NPP circles (like Wright) leaves a vacuum in explaining the legal basis of our justification. Protestant theology rightly maintains that our justification is grounded in the active and passive obedience of Christ.
3.  **The Reality of Legalism**: While Second Temple Judaism had a framework of grace, human nature is inherently prone to legalism (seeking to establish one's own righteousness). Paul’s critique in Romans 10:3 ("seeking to establish their own [righteousness]") includes both ethnic pride and self-righteous performance.
