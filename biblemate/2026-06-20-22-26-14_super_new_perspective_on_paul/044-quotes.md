# Scripture Quotations: Justification, Righteousness of God, and Faith vs. Law

**Persona**: Billy Graham / Verse Scripter  
**Date**: 2026-06-20  

This compilation provides key Old and New Testament scripture passages defining the biblical theology of justification by faith, accompanied by theological connection notes.

---

## 1. Old Testament Foundations

### Genesis 15:6
> **Genesis 15:6 (ESV2016)**  
> *“And he believed the LORD, and he counted it to him as righteousness.”*

*   **Connection Note**: This is the primary theological anchor for Paul’s doctrine of justification. He quotes it in both Romans 4:3 and Galatians 3:6 to prove that Abraham was justified by faith alone, centuries before the Mosaic Law was given and before he was circumcised. It establishes that righteousness is a status credited (*logizomai*) by God to those who trust His promises.

### Habakkuk 2:4
> **Habakkuk 2:4 (ESV2016)**  
> *“Behold, his soul is puffed up; it is not upright within him, but the righteous shall live by his faith.”*

*   **Connection Note**: Cited by Paul in Romans 1:17 and Galatians 3:11. It provides the prophetic link between faith and life. It contrasts the pride of the self-reliant with the loyal trust of the righteous, establishing that faith is the perpetual instrument of covenant life.

### Psalm 32:1-2
> **Psalms 32:1-2 (ESV2016)**  
> *“Blessed is the one whose transgression is forgiven, whose sin is covered. Blessed is the man against whom the LORD counts no iniquity, and in whose spirit there is no deceit.”*

*   **Connection Note**: Cited by Paul in Romans 4:7-8. It demonstrates that the forensic counting of righteousness (justification) includes the non-reckoning of sin (forgiveness). Justification is the legal pardoning of transgressions, where God does not count our iniquities against us.

## 2. New Testament Development

### Romans 1:16-17
> **Romans 1:16-17 (ESV2016)**  
> *“For I am not ashamed of the gospel, for it is the power of God for salvation to everyone who believes, to the Jew first and also to the Greek. For in it the righteousness of God is revealed from faith for faith, as it is written, ‘The righteous shall live by faith.’”*

*   **Connection Note**: The thesis of the Epistle to the Romans. It reveals that the gospel is the power of God that breaks down ethnic barriers ("to the Jew first and also to the Greek") because it reveals God’s righteousness (*dikaiosynē theou*) through the channel of faith.

### Romans 3:20
> **Romans 3:20 (ESV2016)**  
> *“For by works of the law no human being will be justified in his sight, since through the law comes knowledge of sin.”*

*   **Connection Note**: The summary of Paul's indictment. The Law of Moses was never designed to be a path to justification; instead, its diagnostic purpose is to expose human sin and guilt, shutting every mouth before the judgment seat of God.

### Galatians 2:16
> **Galatians 2:16 (ESV2016)**  
> *“yet we know that a person is not justified by works of the law but through faith in Jesus Christ, so we also have believed in Christ Jesus, in order to be justified by faith in Christ and not by works of the law, because by works of the law no one will be justified.”*

*   **Connection Note**: The most concentrated statement of justification by faith in the Pauline corpus. It contrasts *erga nomou* and *pistis Christou* three times, leaving no doubt that salvation is received through Christ's work alone, completely apart from legal or ethnic qualifications.

### Romans 3:28
> **Romans 3:28 (ESV2016)**  
> *“For we hold that one is justified by faith apart from works of the law.”*

*   **Connection Note**: The logical conclusion of Paul's argument in Romans 3. It establishes the Protestant principle of *sola fide* (faith alone) as the sole instrument of justification, excluding any human performance or national privilege.

### Ephesians 2:8-9
> **Ephesians 2:8-9 (ESV2016)**  
> *“For by grace you have been saved through faith. And this is not your own doing; it is the gift of God, not a result of works, so that no one may boast.”*

*   **Connection Note**: A classic summary of the soteriological framework. It states that salvation is entirely by grace (*charis*) through faith (*pistis*). It is a gift of God, completely excluding any human boasting or self-justification, whether moral or ethnic.
