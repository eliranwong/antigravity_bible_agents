# Step 018: Discussion Questions for Small Groups

**Persona**: Compassionate Pastor
**Passage**: Joshua 3

This document contains three thought-provoking discussion questions designed to help small groups apply the theological and pastoral themes of Joshua 3 to their daily lives.

---

### Question 1: Moving Beyond the Wilderness of Survival
*   **Focus**: Identifying comfort zones and wilderness mindsets.
*   **Scripture Connection**: Joshua 3:1-2 (Waiting at the Jordan) and Joshua 3:14 (Leaving the tents).
*   **Discussion Prompt**:
    The Israelites had camped at Shittim and then waited at the Jordan for three days before crossing. The wilderness was a place of familiar, daily provisions (manna), but it was not the destination. For many of us, it is easy to settle for a "wilderness life" of spiritual survival—staying within our comfort zones where we don't have to take risks. 
    *   *What does a "wilderness lifestyle" look like in a modern Christian's life?*
    *   *What are the personal "tents" or wilderness mindsets (e.g., complacency, fear of change, past regrets) that you need to pack up and leave behind to step into the fullness of God's inheritance?*

---

### Question 2: The Practicality of Consecration
*   **Focus**: Sanctification as a prerequisite for seeing God’s power.
*   **Scripture Connection**: Joshua 3:5 (*"Consecrate yourselves, for tomorrow the LORD will do wonders among you."*)
*   **Discussion Prompt**:
    Joshua links our internal spiritual preparation with God's external supernatural action. Consecration (*qadash*) is not about earning God's favor through moral performance, but about cleaning the vessel of our hearts so that He can host His presence in us and work through us.
    *   *In practical, everyday terms, what does it mean to "consecrate yourself" in a culture that constantly pressures us to compromise?*
    *   *For the young adults in our group, how can you concretely take delight in the Lord and guard your hearts against the world's definitions of success and identity?*
    *   *What is one area of your life that the Holy Spirit is asking you to set apart for Him this week?*

---

### Question 3: The Step of Faith-Risk
*   **Focus**: Taking steps of obedience before seeing the outcome.
*   **Scripture Connection**: Joshua 3:15-16 (The river parting only after the priests' feet touch the water).
*   **Discussion Prompt**:
    Unlike the Red Sea crossing where the water parted *before* Israel stepped in, the Jordan River parted only *after* the priests took the risk of walking into the overflowing floodwaters. 
    *   *Why do you think God requires us to take a step of faith-risk before He displays His supernatural power?*
    *   *Can you share a time in your life when you had to "get your feet wet" in obedience to God before you saw Him make a way?*
    *   *What is the "Jordan" in your life right now where you are waiting for the waters to dry up before you take a step, and how can this group support and pray for you to take that first step in faith?*
