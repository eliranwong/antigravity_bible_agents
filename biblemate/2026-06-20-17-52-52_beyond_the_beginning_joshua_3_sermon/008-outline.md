# Step 008: Structural Outline of Joshua 3

**Persona**: Oxford Bible Scholar
**Passage**: Joshua 3

This document presents a detailed homiletical and structural outline of the narrative units within Joshua 3, detailing the exegesis of each division.

---

## I. Section 1: Preparation for the Crossing (Joshua 3:1-6)
*   **v. 1: The Transition Encampment**
    *   Joshua and the assembly rise early, set out from Shittim (site of past apostasy), and arrive at the banks of the Jordan. They lodge (*lûn*) there before crossing, representing a time of waiting and preparation.
*   **vv. 2-4: Instructions for Following the Ark**
    *   At the end of three days, officers go through the camp.
    *   Command: "When you see the ark... set out and follow it."
    *   The requirement of a 2,000-cubit gap. Exegetical significance: The gap maintains the sanctity of the Divine Presence and allows a vast crowd to see their guide through uncharted territory ("for you have not passed this way before").
*   **v. 5: The Command to Consecrate**
    *   Joshua calls the people to "Consecrate yourselves" (*hiṯqaddāšû*).
    *   The promise: "For tomorrow YHWH will do wonders (*niplā’ôṯ*) among you."
*   **v. 6: Commissioning the Priests**
    *   Joshua commands the priests to lift the Ark of the Covenant and march ahead of the people. The priests obey, taking their position as the vanguard.

## II. Section 2: Divine Revelation and Proclamation (Joshua 3:7-13)
*   **vv. 7-8: God's Commission to Joshua**
    *   YHWH promises to exalt Joshua in the eyes of all Israel to confirm His presence with him just as He was with Moses.
    *   Specific instructions: Priests must stand still *in* the Jordan once they reach the brink.
*   **vv. 9-10: Joshua's Address to the Assembly**
    *   Joshua gathers the people: "Come here and listen to the words of YHWH."
    *   Purpose: "By this you shall know (*tēḏe‘ûn*) that the living God (*’ēl ḥay*) is among you" and will dispossess the seven Canaanite nations.
*   **vv. 11-13: The Mechanics of the Miracle Proclaimed**
    *   The Ark of the Lord of all the earth will cross ahead of them.
    *   Command to choose twelve men (one from each tribe) for a future memorial (completed in chapter 4).
    *   The prophecy: When the priests' feet touch the water, the Jordan's flow will be cut off and stand in a single heap (*nēḏ ’eḥāḏ*).

## III. Section 3: The Execution of the Miracle (Joshua 3:14-17)
*   **vv. 14-15: The Moment of Touch**
    *   The people set out from their tents.
    *   The priests carry the Ark to the edge of the river.
    *   Parenthetical note: The Jordan is in flood stage due to harvest season.
    *   The feet of the priests touch (lit. "dip" or "dye" *niṭbəlû*) the water at the brink.
*   **v. 16: The Great Stoppage**
    *   The flowing water from upstream stands still, rising in a heap (*nēḏ ’eḥāḏ*) "very far away, at Adam, the city beside Zarethan."
    *   The downstream water flows to the Salt Sea (Dead Sea) and is "completely cut off."
    *   The people cross opposite Jericho on a dried riverbed.
*   **v. 17: Standing Firm in the Middle**
    *   The priests carrying the Ark stand firmly on dry ground (*ḥārāḇâ*) in the middle of the dry Jordan bed.
    *   They remain stationary, hosting the presence of God in the riverbed, until the entire nation has completed the crossing.
