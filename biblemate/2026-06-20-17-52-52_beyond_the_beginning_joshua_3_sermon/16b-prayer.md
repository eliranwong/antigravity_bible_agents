# Step 016b: Scriptural Prayer based on Joshua 3

**Persona**: Compassionate Pastor
**Passage**: Joshua 3

This document contains a scriptural, pastoral prayer written in the first person, allowing believers to pray these words directly in their personal devotion.

---

## A Prayer of Consecration and Crossing

Heavenly Father, the Lord of all the earth, 

I come before You today at the edge of my own Jordan. You know the barriers that stand in front of me—the flooding waters of anxiety, the deep channels of transition, and the uncertainties of the future. I confess that sometimes I look at the rushing current and become fearful. I have camped at the river’s edge for too long, clinging to the safety of what is familiar, even when You are calling me forward.

Lord, I hear Your call to rise and cross over. I hear Your servant Joshua commanding us: *"As soon as you see the ark of the covenant of the LORD your God... follow it"* (Joshua 3:3). Today, I set my eyes upon You. I turn away from the distractions, the worries, and the opinions of this world, and I fix my gaze on Jesus, the author and perfecter of my faith. Lead me, Lord, for I have not passed this way before. I do not know the path, but I know the Guide.

Holy Spirit, I hear Your command: *"Consecrate yourselves, for tomorrow the LORD will do wonders among you"* (Joshua 3:5). I surrender my life afresh to You. I ask You to wash me, to purify my thoughts, and to set me apart for Your holy purposes. Remove the wilderness mindsets of complaining, fear, and passivity. Cleanse my heart from every compromise, that I might host Your presence with honor and reverence.

Give me the courage, Lord, to step out in faith. Help me to take that risk of obedience, to step into the waters even while they are overflowing their banks. I believe that You are the Living God (*’ēl ḥay*) and that You are in my midst. I trust that as I take the step, You will make a way. 

I thank You that Jesus has already crossed the Jordan of death and judgment on my behalf. He stood firm in the midst of the flood so that I could walk through on dry ground. I declare my total dependence on Your grace, Your power, and Your love. 

Do Your wonders in my life, in my family, and in my church, for the glory of Your holy name.

Amen.
