# Step 017: Sermon Outline and Draft Content

**Persona**: Billy Graham, Bill Johnson, & Compassionate Pastor
**Passage**: Joshua 3
**Title**: Beyond the Beginning

---

## Homiletical Overview
*   **Big Idea**: Entering the fullness of God's inheritance requires keeping our eyes on His presence, consecrating our lives, and taking faith-risks to step into the impossible.
*   **Target Audience**: Mature Christians seeking spiritual breakthrough and young adults looking for dynamic kingdom purpose.
*   **preaching Style**: Earnest, direct, and authoritative (Billy Graham), combined with an emphasis on hosting the Presence, risk-taking, and expecting the supernatural (Bill Johnson).

---

## Sermon Outline

### Introduction: The Threshold of Transition
*   **The Hook**: We all face seasons of transition—standing on the banks of a river that seems too wide, too deep, and too fast to cross. It is the boundary between our past (the wilderness) and our future (the inheritance).
*   **The Text**: Introduce Joshua 3. Contrast the Red Sea (salvation/rescue) with the Jordan (maturity/conquest).
*   **The Proposition**: Today, God is calling you to move *Beyond the Beginning*. He is calling you out of the wilderness of spiritual survival into the land of supernatural promise.

---

### Main Point 1: Aligning Behind the Presence (Joshua 3:1-4)
*   **Exegesis & Historical Background**:
    *   Israel leaves Shittim—the camp associated with their past failure at Baal-Peor (Numbers 25)—and camps at the Jordan for three days. The waiting period is a time of spiritual alignment.
    *   The officers command: *"When you see the ark... follow it"* (v. 3).
    *   The 2,000-cubit gap (about half a mile) is commanded (v. 4). This gap maintains the fear of the Lord and allows the vast crowd to see the Ark clearly.
*   **Theology of hosting the Presence**:
    *   God's presence must lead. We do not ask God to bless our plans; we align ourselves behind His movement. We must become carriers of His presence in our daily lives.
*   **Illustrations**:
    *   *The Guide in the Dark*: Entering a thick, unfamiliar forest. You do not run ahead of the guide; you stay close behind, walking exactly where he walks. Jesus is our Guide through the unknown.
*   **Practical Application**:
    *   We must identify and leave behind our "Shittim"—our places of past compromise. Fix our eyes on Christ through daily devotion, quiet prayer, and absolute surrender.
*   **Punch Line**: "You cannot carry the presence of God into tomorrow if you are carrying the compromises of yesterday."
*   **Transition**: But keeping our eyes on the Presence is only the first step. Once we align behind His movement, we must prepare our hearts. We cannot step into a holy inheritance with unholy lives. This leads us to the second checkpoint: Consecration.

---

### Main Point 2: Consecrating for the Supernatural (Joshua 3:5-13)
*   **Exegesis & Historical Background**:
    *   Joshua commands: *"Consecrate yourselves, for tomorrow the LORD will do wonders among you"* (v. 5). Consecration (*qadash*) is setting oneself apart.
    *   The promise of *niplā’ôṯ* (wonders)—extraordinary, supernatural acts of God.
    *   Joshua announces that the "Living God" (*’ēl ḥay*) is among them, and His presence will dispossess their enemies.
*   **Theology of Sanctification and the Supernatural**:
    *   Holiness is the container for power. God does not manifest His glory in an unaligned temple. Consecration is not about earning God's love; it is about preparing our hearts to receive the weight of His presence.
*   **Addressing Struggles (Mature Believers and Young Adults)**:
    *   *Mature Believers*: Complacency, relying on past victories, or settling for a "wilderness life" of survival.
    *   *Young Adults*: The search for identity and adventure. Concrete delight in YHWH: setting boundaries against cultural compromise, reserving their energy and passions for God’s purposes.
*   **Illustrations**:
    *   *The Clean Vessel*: A surgeon does not use a dirty scalpel, no matter how sharp it is. God uses clean vessels.
*   **Punch Line**: "God is not looking for gold vessels or silver vessels; He is looking for yielded, consecrated vessels."
*   **Transition**: Yet, heart preparation must eventually lead to physical action. Consecration without step-out obedience is merely sentiment. To see the waters part, we must be willing to step in. This brings us to the final climax: the step of faith.

---

### Main Point 3: Stepping into the Impossible (Joshua 3:14-17)
*   **Exegesis & Historical Background**:
    *   The Jordan is overflowing its banks in harvest season. The natural barrier is at its peak.
    *   The priests carry the Ark to the edge. The moment the soles of their feet touch the water, the river parts—standing in a heap at Adam (19 miles away) and drying up at Jericho.
    *   The priests stand firm (*hākên*) in the middle of the riverbed on dry ground (*ḥārāḇâ*) until all have crossed.
*   **Theology of Faith-Risk**:
    *   Supernatural breakthrough occurs at the point of risk. God did not dry up the Jordan while they were in camp; they had to step into the floodwaters first.
*   **Illustrations**:
    *   *The Trapezist*: The flyer must let go of the bar in mid-air before catching the hands of the catcher. Faith is letting go of our visible security.
*   **Gospel Call**:
    *   Jesus Christ is our Great High Priest who walked into the flooding river of death and judgment on the cross. He stood firm under the weight of the wrath of God so that we could cross from death to life on dry ground.
*   **Punch Line**: "If you want to walk on dry ground, you must be willing to get your feet wet."

---

### Conclusion: Conquering the River
*   **Touching Ending**: A pastoral appeal for decision. Will you stay on the wilderness bank, or will you consecrate yourself and step into the river?
*   **Uplifting Encouragement**: God is with you. The same God who split the Red Sea and the Jordan is the Living God who is with you today. Follow the Ark, step out in faith, and watch Him do wonders.
