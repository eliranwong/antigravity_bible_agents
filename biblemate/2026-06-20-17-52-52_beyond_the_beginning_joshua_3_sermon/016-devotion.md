# Step 016: Devotional Reflection on Joshua 3

**Persona**: Billy Graham & Compassionate Pastor
**Passage**: Joshua 3

This document contains a warm pastoral devotional reflection on the spiritual lessons of the Jordan crossing, encouraging the reader to seek personal revival and trust God's leadership.

---

## The Call of the River's Edge

We often find ourselves camping at the edge of our own Jordan. Behind us lies the wilderness—a place where we have known God's provision, but also a place where we have wandered in circles, battling the same old habits, the same old fears, and the same old doubts. Ahead of us lies the Promised Land—the life of abundant fruitfulness, spiritual victory, and deep intimacy with the Lord. 

But between where we are and where God is calling us stands a barrier. And not just any barrier, but a flooding, churning river that seems completely impassable. 

Joshua’s officers walked through the camp and gave a simple command to the people: *"As soon as you see the ark of the covenant of the LORD your God... then you shall set out from your place and follow it"* (Joshua 3:3). The key to crossing the impassable is keeping our eyes fixed on the Presence of God. We must look away from the height of the waves, the swiftness of the current, and the impossibility of our circumstances. We must focus our gaze entirely on the Ark—on the Lord Jesus Christ, who has already gone before us.

Before the miracle could take place, however, the Lord spoke a word that echoes down through the centuries to our hearts today: *"Consecrate yourselves, for tomorrow the LORD will do wonders among you"* (Joshua 3:5). 

My friend, God wants to do wonders in your life. He wants to heal that broken marriage, restore that wayward child, break that binding addiction, and revive your weary soul. But He calls you to consecration. Consecration is the act of setting yourself apart for Him. It is looking into the mirror of God’s Word and saying, "Lord, search me. Purify my heart. Remove anything that stands between me and Your presence." We cannot expect to see the supernatural power of God tomorrow if we are unwilling to yield our lives to Him today.

The crossing of the Jordan was a triumph of faith. The priests carrying the Ark had to walk straight into the rushing water while it was still overflowing its banks. They had to take a step of risk. They had to trust that the God who had promised would be faithful to act. And the moment their feet touched the water, the flow was cut off, and the river stood up in a heap.

Are you willing to put your feet in the water today? Are you willing to take that step of faith, to trust the Savior with your future, and to walk in obedience to His voice? He is the Lord of all the earth, and He will make a way.
