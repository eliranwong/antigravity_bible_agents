# Deep Research Study: The New Perspective on Paul

## Original User Request
Deep research study on Paul New Perspective

## Refined Study Brief
A comprehensive, scholarly examination of the New Perspective on Paul (NPP) — the paradigm shift in Pauline scholarship initiated by E.P. Sanders (*Paul and Palestinian Judaism*, 1977), James D.G. Dunn ('The New Perspective on Paul,' 1983), and N.T. Wright. This study will:

1. **Trace the historical development** of the NPP from its precursors to its current forms
2. **Examine the key Pauline texts** central to the debate (Galatians 2:15-21; Romans 3:20-28; Romans 4; Romans 9:30-10:13; Philippians 3:2-11; Ephesians 2:8-10)
3. **Analyze the contested Greek terminology** at the heart of the controversy: ἔργα νόμου (*erga nomou*, 'works of the law'), δικαιοσύνη θεοῦ (*dikaiosynē theou*, 'righteousness of God'), πίστις Χριστοῦ (*pistis Christou*, 'faith of/in Christ')
4. **Engage with both traditional Reformed and NPP readings** of justification, covenant membership, and the role of Torah
5. **Assess theological implications** for soteriology, ecclesiology, and the relationship between Old and New Testaments
6. **Provide a balanced, theologically faithful conclusion** that honours both the contributions and the limitations of the NPP
