# Scriptural Intercessory Prayer: Union and Peace in Christ (Galatians 2:20 & Romans 5:1-2)

**Persona**: Compassionate Pastor  
**Date**: 2026-06-20  

This prayer is drafted in the first person so it can be prayed directly, grounding the theological realities of justification and union with Christ in personal devotion and intercession.

---

### Adoration
*O Lord God, the One who is faithful to Your covenant promises from generation to generation, I praise You. I adore You for Your infinite justice and Your unfathomable mercy. You are the God who justifies the ungodly, the One who brings life out of death and calls into existence things that do not exist. I praise You for revealing Your saving righteousness through the Lord Jesus Christ. You are the sovereign Patron of my soul, bestowing grace as a free, unearned, and glorious gift. I exalt You for Your holy character, which is perfectly demonstrated at the cross of Calvary.*

### Confession
*Father, I come to You in humility, acknowledging my constant temptation to seek justification apart from You. I confess that I have often sought to establish my own righteousness, relying on my performance, my moral efforts, and my cultural pride. I have strived under the law of works, seeking the approval of others and trying to buy Your favor, which has only led to exhaustion and spiritual dryness. I confess that I have allowed ethnic, social, or cultural boundary markers to divide my heart from my brothers and sisters in Christ. Forgive me for my pride, my self-reliance, and my lack of trust in Your finished work. Cleanse me of all self-justification, and silence the voice of the accuser in my heart.*

### Thanksgiving
*My heart overflows with gratitude, O God, for the glorious truth of the gospel. I thank You that, having been justified by faith, I now have peace with You through my Lord Jesus Christ (Rom 5:1). I thank You that the hostility has been ended, and that I stand in a relationship of reconciliation, secure in Your family. I express my deepest gratitude that, through Him, I have obtained access by faith into this grace in which I now stand, and that I can rejoice in the hope of the glory of God (Rom 5:2). Thank You that I have been crucified with Christ, and that it is no longer I who live, but Christ who lives in me (Gal 2:20). Thank You that the life I now live in the flesh, I live by faith in the Son of God, who loved me and gave Himself up for me (Gal 2:20).*

### Supplication
*Lord, I petition You for deep personal transformation. Teach me to walk daily in the reality of my crucifixion and resurrection with Christ. Let His love compel me, and let His life be manifested in my actions, my speech, and my thoughts. Transform my heart from fear to love, from self-righteousness to radical humility. 
I pray for Your church. Tear down the walls of division that separate us. Let us eat at the same table in unity and peace, reflecting the multi-ethnic family You promised to Abraham. Deliver us from legalism, and protect us from ethnocentrism. Finally, I pray for the world. Let the message of justification by grace through faith alone go forth with power, bringing freedom to the captive, healing to the brokenhearted, and reconciliation to the nations. In the mighty and precious name of Jesus Christ, my Savior. Amen.*
