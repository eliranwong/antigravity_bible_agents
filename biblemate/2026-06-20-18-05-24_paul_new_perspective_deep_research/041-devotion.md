# Devotional Reflection: The Heart of the Gospel (Romans 3:21-26)

**Persona**: Billy Graham / Compassionate Pastor  
**Date**: 2026-06-20  

*“But now the righteousness of God has been manifested apart from the law, although the Law and the Prophets bear witness to it— the righteousness of God through faith in Jesus Christ for all who believe.”* — Romans 3:21-22 (ESV2016)

---

## The Turning Point of History

My friends, there is no more glorious transition in all of Scripture than the words that open Romans 3:21: **“But now.”** 

Before these words, the Apostle Paul has painted a dark and desperate picture of the human condition. He has shown us that every one of us—no matter our background, our education, or our moral efforts—stands guilty before a holy and righteous God. The Bible says that “all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God” (Rom 3:23). We are bankrupt, unable to pay our debt, and powerless to save ourselves. If we try to stand before God on our own merits or our own "works of the law," we will fail. 

“But now” the light has broken into our darkness! God, in His infinite love and mercy, has done for us what we could never do for ourselves. He has revealed a way to be made right with Him that does not depend on our performance or our heritage. It is the righteousness of God, and it comes to us as a free, undeserved gift through faith in Jesus Christ.

---

## The Great Exchange at the Cross

How is this possible? How can a holy and just God declare guilty sinners to be righteous without compromising His own justice? 

The answer is found at the cross. Paul tells us that God put forward Jesus Christ to be a **“propitiation by his blood”** (Rom 3:25). On that dark Friday, Jesus took your place and my place. He bore the wrath of God against sin. He became the sacrifice that satisfied the justice of God, so that the mercy of God could be poured out on all who believe. 

Think of it: at the cross, the ultimate exchange took place. Christ took our sin, our shame, and our death; in return, He gives us His righteousness, His peace, and His eternal life. It is a gift of pure grace. You cannot buy it, you cannot earn it, you can only receive it with open hands of faith.

---

## Walking in the Freedom of Grace

Are you still trying to earn God’s favor? Are you still carrying the heavy burden of guilt, wondering if you have done enough, prayed enough, or been good enough? 

My friend, look to the cross. Jesus has paid it all. The work of salvation is finished. Today, God is calling you to stop striving and start resting in His finished work. Lay down your self-righteousness, your pride, and your fears, and trust in Jesus Christ alone.

### Reflection Questions
1.  In what areas of your life are you still trying to "earn" your righteousness or prove your worth to God and others?
2.  How does the truth that Christ is your *hilastērion* (mercy seat) change how you view your past mistakes and guilt?
3.  How can you live today as a person who is defined by the free gift of God’s righteousness?

---

## Pastoral Prayer of Reconciliation

*Lord God, I come before You today acknowledging my deep and desperate need for Your grace. I confess that I have sinned, and that I fall far short of Your glory. I have tried so often to establish my own righteousness, relying on my works, my moral efforts, and my performance, only to find myself exhausted and empty.*

*But today, I look to the cross of Jesus Christ. I thank You, Father, that You put Him forward as the propitiation for my sins. I thank You that His blood was shed for me, paying the debt I could never pay. I receive the free gift of Your righteousness by faith. I trust in Jesus alone as my Savior and my Lord.*

*Fill me with Your Holy Spirit. Transform my heart, so that I may live no longer for myself, but for Him who loved me and gave Himself for me. Give me the grace to love others with the same unconditional love and acceptance that You have extended to me. In the precious name of Jesus, I pray. Amen.*
